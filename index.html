Objective Stats
Goals (G)
Shots that score.
Assists (A)
Pass that directly leads to a teammate's goal.
Shots on goal (SOG)
Shots that hit the net (goal or save).
Shots off goal
Shots that miss the net or hit the pipe.
Free position
Shot taken on a free position restart — goals (FPG) over attempts (FPA).
Ground balls won (GB)
Possessions secured from loose balls.
Caused TOs (CT)
Defender forces the opposing player to turn the ball over.
Interceptions
Defender picks off an opponent's pass.
Draw controls
Possession won directly off the draw at the center circle.
Clears
Successful (SUC) transitions of the ball from the defensive half to the offensive half, over attempts (ATT).
Saves
Shots stopped by the goalie that would have been goals.
Goals allowed (GA)
Shots scored against the goalie.
Subjective Stats (Coach-Graded)
GB Missed
An uncontested ground ball with no immediate pressure or impediment on the pickup that the player failed to secure. Missed groundballs under defensive pressure do not count — this stat only captures "should-have-had-it" effort/focus plays.
DSR — Dodge Success Rate
Percentage of 1v1 dodge attempts (from left or right) where the ball carrier created a meaningful offensive advantage. A dodge is successful when the player creates clear separation, gains a full step, forces the defense to slide, generates a shot attempt, or forces a defensive help rotation. A dodge is unsuccessful when the player is kept in front, fails to create space, doesn't draw a slide, resets the offense without threatening the goal, or turns the ball over on the dodge.
DSR = (DL-S + DR-S) / (DL-A + DR-A)
DWR — Defensive Win Rate
Percentage of 1v1 dodges faced where the defender neutralized the attacker without requiring help. A dodge is blocked when the defender stays with the ball carrier, forces a reset/pass-out with no advantage, prevents a slide from being needed, forces a contested low-quality shot, or causes the turnover directly. DWR measures 1v1 defense only — if a slide is required, only the original defender takes the loss; what happens after the slide is team defense, graded separately.
DWR = dodges blocked / times dodged on
GSO — Scoring Opportunity Created
A quality scoring chance that reasonably should have produced a shot — credited to the player who RECEIVES the opportunity, not the player who creates it. Counts when the receiver takes a shot on goal, takes a shot off net from a quality position, drops a catchable pass in a scoring position, turns the ball over (unforced) in a scoring position, or misses a catchable feed in the middle of the field with room to shoot. The test: "Did she have a realistic chance to score?"
Unforced TO
Turnover caused by the player herself (miscradle, bad decision, stepping out of bounds) with no defensive pressure.
Pressured TO
Turnover caused by opposing defensive pressure on this player — a strip, a forced bad pass, or a checked shot. Distinct from Caused TOs, which is the opposite (this player forcing the opponent's turnover).
Caused TOs (CT) — context
This player forced the opposing team into a turnover. Good for us. Opposite of Pressured TO (which is the opponent doing it to us).
Dodge hand tendency
Shown when a player attempts meaningfully more dodges going one direction. Indicates the side defenders should overplay. Only displays when total dodge attempts exceed 2 and attempts are uneven.
Composite Metrics
Shot %
Finishing rate on all shot attempts.
goals / (SOG + shots off goal)
Pass %
Completion rate on pass attempts.
passes completed / passes attempted
Draw ctrl %
Success rate on contested draws.
DC-W / (DC-W + DC-L)
Ast/TO ratio
Assists per turnover — measures decision quality. Both unforced and pressured turnovers count against; caused turnovers do not.
assists / (unforced TO + pressured TO)
Save %
Goalie's rate of stopping shots on goal.
saves / (saves + goals allowed)
Radar chart rules
Shows a player's skill shape across up to 5 axes: Shot %, Pass %, DSR %, Draw ctrl %, DWR %. Any axis with zero attempts (no denominator) is hidden. If fewer than 3 axes qualify, the radar is hidden entirely and the player's identity is communicated through stats and blurb only.
